# Pulsatance Brand Guidelines

## Brand Name & Meaning

**pul·sa·tance** /pəlˈsātəns/  
*noun • Physics (archaic)*

### Definitions

1. **Scientific:** Angular velocity; the rate at which angular position changes with respect to time.

2. **Interpretive:** The capacity to pivot direction without losing velocity—maintaining forward energy while reorienting trajectory.

### Etymology
From Latin *pulsare* (to beat, to strike) + *-ance* (state or quality of). Mid-19th century term largely superseded by "angular velocity" in modern usage.

### Strategic Application
In business contexts: the ability to change direction while maintaining momentum—to reorient competitive positioning, product focus, or organizational structure while preserving forward energy and market velocity.

---

## Core Essence

*"What if a brilliant 1960s physicist wrote a textbook about your company's future?"*

Pulsatance channels the optimistic rationalism of mid-century science—when elegant diagrams could explain complex systems, when clarity was prized over jargon, and when intellectual playfulness coexisted with rigorous thinking. It's Feynman explaining angular momentum on a chalkboard, then helping you figure out where your biotech company needs to pivot.

**Backgrounds:**

- Strategic vectors: Coral (#ff6b6b)

### Principle of Restraint

The power of this palette comes from discipline. Let the grayscale do most of the work—it conveys precision, clarity, and intellectual rigor. Reserve coral for moments of strategic importance: directional change, key insights, calls to action. When coral appears, it should feel deliberate and meaningful.

## Visual Identity

### Design Aesthetic
**The 1960s Scientific Textbook**

Physical artifact reference: Scientific textbook from the 1960s with figures labeled "Fig. 3.2"

### Core Visual Elements

**Primary Motif:**
- Angular velocity diagrams
- Literal rotation arrows, vectors, phase spaces
- ω (omega) symbol as visual shorthand

**Secondary Elements:**
- Vintage scientific illustration style
- Biological oscillations, cell cycle diagrams
- Systems dynamics visualizations

**Treatment:**
- Clean diagrams with subtle hand-drawn qualities
- Precise but not sterile
- Technical clarity with soul
- Slightly analog/imperfect—embrace warmth over perfection

### Design Principles

1. **Embrace slight imperfection** — warmth over polish
2. **Show your work** — let the thinking be visible
3. **Geometric forms with organic content** — vectors describing biological rhythms
4. **Intentionally 1960s** — period-authentic without irony

### Textbook Aesthetic in Practice

**Literal applications:**
- Website sections titled like chapters: "Chapter 3: On Competitive Dynamics"
- Diagrams numbered and captioned: "Fig. 2.1 - Product-market phase space"
- Footer text styled like textbook citations: "Pulsatance (2025). Angular Strategies for Biotech Innovation."
- Case studies presented as "worked examples" with step-by-step breakdowns

**Physical materials:**
- Business cards: Thick, slightly textured stock (not glossy)
- Proposals: Bound like course materials, not slick pitch decks
- One-pagers: Designed like a pulled page from a textbook—generous margins for annotations
- Letterhead: Simple geometric header, feels like departmental stationery

### Embracing Analog Imperfection

**Where to add warmth:**
- Hand-drawn arrow annotations on diagrams (even if digitally rendered)
- Slightly irregular line weights—not laser-precision CAD
- Scanned texture overlays—paper grain, slight ink variation
- Diagrams that look sketched on graph paper first, then refined
- Allow for "marginalia"—small notes, asterisks, thoughtful asides

**What stays precise:**
- Typography (clean, geometric, but not cold)
- Grid system (rational, spacious)
- Technical terminology (accurate, specific)
- The thinking itself (rigorous, systematic)

The warmth comes from **how** you present precise thinking, not from compromising the precision itself.

## Voice & Tone

### Hierarchy of Qualities

1. **Approachable and conversational** — Explain complex things clearly, invite curiosity
2. **Precise and technical** — Know the science, use proper terms
3. **Poetic and evocative** — Find the elegant metaphor, the illuminating connection
4. **Confident and direct** — But quietly so, no overselling needed

### What This Sounds Like

- **Feynman's clarity:** "Let me show you why this is actually simple"
- **Systems thinking:** "Here's how these pieces move together"
- **Optimistic rationalism:** "We can figure this out"
- **No consulting-speak:** Avoid "leverage," "synergies," "disruption"
- **No academic heaviness:** Skip the credential-dropping
- **No startup theater:** No hustle, no hype

### Analog Imperfection in Voice

**Not:** Ultra-polished consultant-speak ("We leverage best-in-class frameworks...")  
**Not:** Overly warm/casual ("Hey! Let's chat about your strategy!")  
**But:** Warmly rigorous ("Let's map out what's actually happening here.")

**Voice characteristics:**
- Using "you" and "your" naturally, not "one must consider"
- Starting sentences with "So" or "Now" when it flows naturally
- Allowing conversational asides in parentheses
- Occasional incomplete sentences when they're clearer than complete ones
- But: Still precise with technical terms, still structured in thinking

Think: Professor explaining something fascinating at office hours, not lecturing from a podium.

## Strategic Positioning

### What Pulsatance Is NOT

- ❌ **Corporate consulting** — McKinsey polish, generic frameworks
- ❌ **Tech startup culture** — Disruption theater, move-fast-break-things
- ❌ **Academic consultancy** — CV-forward, overly formal
- ❌ **Innovation theater** — Tired metaphors, stock photography

### What Pulsatance IS

- ✓ The polymathic strategist who thinks in systems
- ✓ The person who can diagram your dynamics on a whiteboard and make it all make sense
- ✓ Mid-century optimism meets modern biotech complexity
- ✓ Where physics principles illuminate biological and business problems
- ✓ Intellectual curiosity as a service

### Emotional Resonance for Clients

When a biotech CEO encounters the brand, they should feel:
- **Intellectual curiosity:** "This person thinks differently than other consultants"
- **Creative energy:** "They'll help us see patterns we're missing"
- "Creative energy but grounded in rigor"
- "Finally, someone who gets both the science AND the strategy"

### Intellectual Archetypes

**Richard Feynman** — Playful brilliance, clarity through simplicity

The brand channels Feynman's approach: making complex ideas accessible through elegant explanation, maintaining intellectual rigor while being approachable, and finding joy in the process of understanding.

- Hand-drawn diagrams
- Vintage scientific illustrations

- Slightly analog feel

### Content Approach

- Lead with curious questions
- Use diagrams to explain
- Reference unexpected connections (physics → biology → strategy)
- Be precise about what you mean
- Show the elegance of the solution

### The "Pulsatance Diagram" Style

A recognizable approach to explaining client challenges:
- Gray framework/axes
- Coral vectors showing forces, directions, momentum
- Clean labels in geometric sans
- Hand-drawn quality to the arrows
- Numbered annotations: "1. Current position 2. Market forces 3. Strategic pivot point"

**Example applications:**
- "Fig. 1.3 - Your competitive landscape as a phase space"
- "Fig. 4.7 - Product cycles and market oscillations"
- "Fig. 2.9 - Organizational velocity vectors"

## Brand Summary

**The Vibe in One Sentence:**  
Optimistic mid-century rationalism applied to modern biotech strategy—playful brilliance, elegant systems thinking, and the confidence that complex dynamics can be understood and navigated.

**For Claude:**  
When creating content for Pulsatance, embody a 1960s physics professor who's genuinely excited about helping biotech companies solve strategic puzzles. Be warm and rigorous. Use diagrams. Explain clearly. Never oversell. Let the thinking speak for itself.
